Tunable Comb Filter

Publication: A New CFOA-Based Shadow Analog Comb Filter (JAPED, 2020)

This project introduces a novel shadow comb filter based on the concept of current feedback operational amplifier (CFOA). It combines multiple notch filters with different center frequencies. The bandwidth and quality factor of each filter is controlled via the gain of the amplifier using a single variable resistor. This design enables independent electronic tuning of each notch's bandwidth without affecting the center frequency or the overall gain. The notch filters are centered around 1 kHz, 10 kHz, 100 kHz, and 1 MHz and implemented using the commercially available AD844 ICs.

Notch Filter Design

Core equations governing the design:

Center frequency:
\[ f₀ = \frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{R_1 R_3 C_1 C_2}} \]

Bandwidth: (when \( R_a = R_1 = R \))
\[ BW = \frac{1}{C_2 R_2} - \frac{R_b}{R_a C_2 R_1} = \frac{1}{R C_2} \left(\frac{R}{R_2} - \frac{R_b}{R}\right) \]

Experimental Results

Experiments validated smooth electronic bandwidth tuning without affecting the notch frequency. Adjusting a single variable resistor (with inverse control over R2 and Rb) effectively modified the bandwidth. The measured notch depths at different frequencies were:

1 kHz → -56 dB
10 kHz → -55 dB
100 kHz → -46 dB
1 MHz → -36 dB
