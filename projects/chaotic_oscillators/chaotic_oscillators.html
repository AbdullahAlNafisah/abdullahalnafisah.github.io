Chaotic Oscillators for Secure Communication

Publications: MWSCAS 2022 • JAPED 2020 • IJEECS 2019

Leveraging chaos theory and circuits such as Chua’s circuit, this project implements chaotic oscillators using memristor-based Wien-bridge and two-transistor RC designs. Their aperiodic yet deterministic dynamics, sensitivity to initial conditions, broad frequency content (natural spread spectrum), and ability to synchronize via capacitive coupling make them attractive for analog scrambling/masking and recovery in secure links. We experimentally demonstrate synchronization (time/frequency/phase) and audio masking with successful recovery under proper gain tuning.

Memristor-Based Wien-Bridge Oscillator

A standard Wien-bridge is modified by replacing a resistor with a memristor emulator to induce nonlinearity for chaos. The oscillation frequency and sustainability follow the relations in the model; the time-varying memristor resistance \(R_M(t)\) drives the chaotic regime.

Two-Transistor Chaotic Oscillator

A compact RC oscillator using two NPN transistors (e.g., 2N4401) achieves chaos without inductors. Biasing and RC feedback shape a new equilibrium; tuning a variable resistor adjusts the base frequency in the chaotic regime.

Synchronization & Secure Analog Masking

Master and slave oscillators are capacitively coupled (\(C_{\text{Syn}}\)) so the slave tracks the master. An input \(V_{\text{IN}}\) is masked by summing with the chaotic carrier; at the receiver, subtraction with the synchronized chaotic signal recovers the message when gains are matched.
