Circuit Theory

This is a summary to refresh circuit theory topics.

Highlights
  • Ohm's law: \(V = IR\)
  • Kirchhoff's circuit laws
    • KCL: algebraic sum of currents at a node is zero ( \( \sum_{i=0}^n I_i = 0 \) ).
    • KVL: algebraic sum of voltages around a loop is zero ( \( \sum_{i=0}^n V_i = 0 \) ).
  • Thevenin & Norton Theorems: Linear electrical network containing only voltage sources, current sources and resistances can be replaced by an equivalent combination of a voltage source Vth or current source Ino in a series or parallel connection with a resistance Rth= Rno repectively."

Circuit Variables and Elements
  • Variables: voltage \(v(t)\), current \(i(t)\); power \(p(t) = v(t)i(t)\), energy \(w(t) = \int p\,dt\).
  • Passive sign convention; dependent vs independent sources (V/I, controlled by V/I).
  • Elements: R, L, C with \(v=iR\), \(v=L\,di/dt\), \(i=C\,dv/dt\).
Resistive Circuits
  • Series/parallel combinations, voltage/current dividers.
  • Source transformations: (Vth ↔ In).
  • Maximum power transfer: \(R_L = R_{th}\) (DC resistive).
Divider quick check
Circuit Analysis
  • Methods: Nodal (KCL) and mesh (KVL) analysis; superposition; Thevenin/Norton.
  • Guideline: many voltage sources → mesh; many current sources → nodal.
KCL/KVL sanity check
Op-amp
  • Ideal rules: \(v_+ \approx v_-\) (with negative feedback), input currents ≈ 0, finite output swing.
  • Common circuits: Inverting/non-inverting/summing; integrator/differentiator (with practical stabilization).
  • VFA vs CFA: bandwidth depends on \(R_f\) for CFAs.
Op-amp gain quick calc
Inductance, Capacitance, and Mutual Inductance
  • Energy: \(w_L=\tfrac12 L i^2\), \(w_C=\tfrac12 C v^2\).
  • Impedances: \(Z_L=j\omega L\), \(Z_C=1/(j\omega C)\). Reactance magnitudes \(|X_L|=\omega L\), \(|X_C|=1/(\omega C)\).
  • Mutual inductance: \(v_1=L_1\,di_1/dt + M\,di_2/dt\), dot convention.
1st-order RL and RC Circuits (Responses)
  • Time constants: \(\tau_{RC}=RC\), \(\tau_{RL}=L/R\).
  • Step responses: \(v_C(t)=V_f+(V_i-V_f)e^{-t/RC}\); \(i_L(t)=I_f+(I_i-I_f)e^{-tR/L}\).
Time constant & step value
Natural and Step Responses of RLC Circuits
  • Parameters: Series RLC: \(\alpha=R/(2L)\), \(\omega_0=1/\sqrt{LC}\), \(\zeta=\alpha/\omega_0\).
  • Classification: underdamped (\(\zeta<1\)), critically damped (\(\zeta=1\)), overdamped (\(\zeta>1\)).
RLC classifier
Sinusoidal Steady-State Analysis and Power Calculations
  • Phasors: \(v(t)=\Re\{\tilde V e^{j\omega t}\}\), Ohm in phasor domain \(\tilde V=\tilde I Z\).
  • Complex power: \(S=VI^* = P + jQ\); power factor \(\cos\varphi=P/|S|\).
AC power calculator
Balanced Three-Phase Circuits
  • Y connection: \(V_{ph}=V_L/\sqrt{3}\), \(I_L=I_{ph}\).
  • Δ connection: \(V_{ph}=V_L\), \(I_L=\sqrt{3}I_{ph}\).
  • Total power: \(P=\sqrt{3}V_L I_L\cos\varphi\); \(Q=\sqrt{3}V_L I_L\sin\varphi\).
3ϕ quick calculator
Laplace Transform Circuit Analysis
  • Impedances: \(Z_R=R\), \(Z_L = sL\), \(Z_C = 1/(sC)\).
  • Method: initial conditions become sources; solve in \(s\)-domain, then inverse Laplace.
Common transforms
Frequency Selective Circuits
  • RC low/high-pass: \(f_c=\dfrac{1}{2\pi RC}\).
  • RLC resonance: \(f_0=\dfrac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{LC}}\), \(Q=\dfrac{1}{R}\sqrt{\dfrac{L}{C}}\).
Corner / resonance calculator
Active Filter Circuits
  • Sallen–Key (unity-gain, equal parts): \(f_c=\dfrac{1}{2\pi RC}\), \(Q=1/2\).
  • Multiple-feedback: used for higher Q and band-pass designs.

Fourier Series & Transform
  • Square wave (±A, 50% duty): odd harmonics only, \(b_n=\dfrac{4A}{n\pi}\) for odd \(n\).
  • Properties: linearity, time/frequency shift, convolution.
