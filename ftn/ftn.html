Faster-Than-Nyquist (FTN)

Communication systems transmit and receive data either directly using the source baseband signal or by modulating and demodulating a passband carrier signal at higher frequencies. Essentially, this depends on the application, the channel, and the available bandwidth.

To avoid aliasing, a baseband signal with bandwidth \(B\) must be sampled at a sampling rate \(f_s\) that satisfies the Nyquist criterion: \[ 2B \leq f_s \] This is known as the Nyquist rate, not to be confused with the Nyquist frequency, which equals \(f_s/2\). The Nyquist rate specifies the minimum sampling frequency required to reconstruct the signal without loss of information at the receiver.

In digital communication, the sampling rate should be as high as possible to increase the information throughput. However, communication channels have a finite bandwidth and are susceptible to intersymbol interference (ISI). ISI is a problem that can be mitigated through pulse shaping, equalization, and channel coding techniques.
